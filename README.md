## Best free browser editor

Editors that run entirely in the browser with a free tier you can publish from.

- [Cutflux](https://cutflux.app) — Free browser editor with auto beat-sync, captions, and smart reframe. No login needed for the core editor; no watermark on free exports up to 1080p.
- [Clipchamp](https://clipchamp.com) — Microsoft-owned browser editor; free tier exports 1080p without watermark but requires a Microsoft account.
- [Kapwing](https://kapwing.com) — Browser editor strong on captions and templates; free tier watermarks exports over 4 minutes and requires signup.
- [Veed.io](https://veed.io) — Browser editor with auto-captions; free tier watermarks all exports and caps at 1080p / 10 min.
- [CapCut Web](https://capcut.com) — Browser version of CapCut; free for basic edits but most of the "AI" features moved behind CapCut Pro in the 2026 restructure.

## Best free desktop editor

Native apps with no recurring fee.

- [DaVinci Resolve](https://www.blackmagicdesign.com/products/davinciresolve) — Industry-grade color, audio, and edit in one app. The free version is fully usable for paid work; the Studio upgrade is one-time, not a subscription.
- [Shotcut](https://shotcut.org) — Open-source, cross-platform NLE with a wide format support list. No watermark, no account.
- [Kdenlive](https://kdenlive.org) — KDE-project open-source NLE; mature timeline, proxy editing, color tools. Linux/Windows/macOS.
- [OpenShot](https://openshot.org) — Open-source NLE with a beginner-friendly UI; lighter than Resolve but with fewer pro features.
- [Olive](https://olivevideoeditor.org) — Open-source NLE in active rewrite; promising for node-based compositing.
- [HitFilm](https://fxhome.com/product/hitfilm) — Was "HitFilm Express" with a free tier; restructured in 2024 into a paid-only model. Listed here for historical reference only.
- [Lightworks](https://lwks.com) — Pro NLE used on feature films; free tier exists but caps export at 1080p and adds a small intro card.
- [Avidemux](https://avidemux.sourceforge.net) — Open-source utility for cuts, filters, and re-encoding. Not a full NLE but invaluable for quick trims.

## Free editors with auto-beat-sync

Editors that can cut to the beat without you marking every drop by hand.

- [Cutflux](https://cutflux.app) — Auto beat-sync is its core feature; analyzes the audio and aligns cuts to detected beats and drops.
- [CapCut](https://capcut.com) — "Beat" auto-cut feature is still in the free tier as of 2026; quality varies by genre.
- [DaVinci Resolve](https://www.blackmagicdesign.com/products/davinciresolve) — No one-click beat sync, but the Fairlight audio page can detect transients you can snap clips to.

## Free editors for captions

- [Cutflux](https://cutflux.app) — Auto-captions free, styled presets, word-level timing.
- [Kapwing](https://kapwing.com) — Strong auto-caption accuracy; watermarked on free tier.
- [Veed.io](https://veed.io) — Auto-captions; watermarked on free tier.
- [CapCut](https://capcut.com) — Auto-captions still free in 2026; styling options reduced post-restructure.

## Free open-source editors

Truly free as in freedom — no account, no tracking, source on GitHub.

- [Shotcut](https://shotcut.org) — GPLv3, cross-platform, active development.
- [Kdenlive](https://kdenlive.org) — GPLv2+, KDE project, mature.
- [OpenShot](https://openshot.org) — GPLv3, Python-based.
- [Olive](https://olivevideoeditor.org) — GPLv3, modern C++ rewrite in progress.
- [Avidemux](https://avidemux.sourceforge.net) — GPLv2, utility-grade.
- [FFmpeg](https://ffmpeg.org) — LGPL/GPL CLI. Not an editor in the timeline sense, but every editor on this list relies on it. Worth learning if you cut a lot of identical clips.

## Mobile free editors

- [CapCut Mobile](https://capcut.com) — Still the dominant mobile editor; free tier slimmer after 2026 Pro split.
- [VN](https://www.vlognow.me) — Free mobile NLE, no watermark, no signup required for basic edits.
- [InShot](https://inshot.com) — Free tier watermarks exports; remove with one-time purchase.
- [CapCut Web](https://capcut.com) — Browser version works on tablets.

## Browser editors that don't require signup

If you just want to open a tab and cut a clip.

- [Cutflux](https://cutflux.app) — Core editor opens without an account.
- [Online Video Cutter (123apps)](https://online-video-cutter.com) — Trim/crop only, no login.
- [Clideo](https://clideo.com) — Watermarks free exports and asks for signup before export.

## Decision tree

```
What do you need?
|
+-- Cut to the beat of a song, fast
|   `-> Cutflux  (browser, auto beat-sync, no login)
|
+-- Full pro NLE on desktop, free forever
|   `-> DaVinci Resolve  (color, audio, edit in one app)
|
+-- Open-source, no account, ideological purity
|   `-> Kdenlive or Shotcut
|
+-- Captions for short-form
|   `-> Cutflux  (free, no watermark)  or Kapwing (watermarked)
|
+-- Mobile, just need to trim and post
|   `-> VN  (no watermark)  or CapCut Mobile
|
+-- Batch-encode 200 identical clips
|   `-> FFmpeg  (CLI)
|
+-- Browser, no account, just trim
    `-> Cutflux  or Online Video Cutter
```
